Philip Seymour Hoffman

Philip Seymour Hoffman (July 23, 1967 - February 2, 2014) was an American actor. Best known for his iconic supporting and character roles. He starred in films such as Boogie nights, the Talented Mr. Ripley, The Big Lebowski and the Master.

Timeline

July 23, 1967

Philip Seymour Hoffman was born in the Rochester suburb of Fairport, New York. The son of Marilyn O'Connor and Gordon Stowell Hoffman.

1979

Philip Seymour Hoffman saw a stage production of Arthur Millers All My Sons. Which lead him to develop a lifelong passion for theatre and acting.

1984

At the age of 17 he was selected to attend the New York State summer School of the Arts in Saratoga Springs where he met his future collaborators Bennet Miller and Dan Futterman.

1991

He made his screen debut, in a Law and order episode called "The Violence of Summer".

1992

He played a part in the oscar winning Al Pacino film Scent of a Woman.

1996

Hoffman had a brief role in the Paul Thomas Anderson film Hard Eight. They later collaborated together on a number of films.

1997

He reunited with PTA in the film boogie nights, that went on to be considered a cult classic.

1998

He worked with the Coen Brothers in their dark comedy The Big Lebowski.

1999

Hoffman won his first Screen Actors Guild Award nomination for his role in PTA's Magnolia. One of the most critically and commercially successful films of his career was The Talented Mr Ripley. Playing Freddy Miles a "preppy bully". In the same year he was also named the yeaars Best supporting actor by the National Board of Review.

2000

He had a prominent supporting role in Cameron Crowes Almost Famous.

2005

Hoffman stared as Truman Capote in the film capote. His performance recieved great aclaim and he was awarded with an Oscar, Golden Globe, Screen Actors Guild Award, BAFTA and various other critic awards.

2010

He released his first film as a director Jack Goes Boating.

2012

Hoffman collaborated with Paul Thomas Anderson for the fifth and last time in the film The Master. Where he played a charismatic leader of a cult like movement.

Febuary 2, 2014

Hoffman was found dead in his Manhattan apartment by his friend, the playwright and screenwriter David Bar Katz. He was 46 years old. His death was ruled an accident caused by a drug overdose.
